# Humanize

Use this skill to remove AI-writing tells without flattening the text into something bland.

## Core job

Do four things:
1. Keep the meaning.
2. Remove obvious AI patterns.
3. Keep or restore a real voice.
4. Make the result sound like something a person would actually send.

Do not just make the text shorter or simpler. Make it feel written.

## What to look for

Cut or rewrite these patterns when they show up:
- inflated significance language
- fake depth and abstract "broader meaning"
- generic praise or promotional tone
- overly tidy structure
- repetitive sentence rhythm
- rule-of-three writing
- empty contrast lines like "not just X, but Y"
- em dash overuse
- signposting like "let's break this down"
- filler CTAs like "if you'd like, I can..."
- vague corporate words like "compelling," "powerful," "meaningful," "valuable," unless they are earned
- writing that explains itself instead of just saying the thing

## Default rewrite style

When no voice sample is provided, aim for writing that is:
- natural
- specific
- a little opinionated when appropriate
- rhythmically varied
- less symmetrical
- more direct

Prefer:
- shorter openings
- cleaner verbs
- concrete nouns
- fewer stacked qualifiers
- occasional sharpness over smoothness

## Voice matching

If the user gives a writing sample, match it.

Check:
- sentence length
- paragraph shape
- punctuation habits
- level of casualness
- whether they sound dry, warm, blunt, playful, or formal
- recurring phrases or habits

Then rewrite into that voice instead of using a generic "good writing" voice.

## Process

When humanizing text:
1. Read the full passage once for intent.
2. Mark the lines that sound most artificial.
3. Rewrite for voice and rhythm, not just correctness.
4. Read it again as if it were a real email, DM, memo, or paragraph spoken aloud.
5. Do a final anti-AI pass with these two checks:
   - "What still makes this sound AI-written?"
   - "Now make it not sound AI-written."

## Output format

Unless the user asks for something else, provide:
1. a revised version
2. a brief note on what changed, only if useful

If the user asks for alternatives, provide 2-3 distinct versions rather than tiny variations.

## Special guidance for outreach and copy

For outreach, cold emails, DMs, and persuasive copy:
- remove generic flattery
- remove startup-pitch energy
- avoid over-explaining the product
- avoid sounding like a template with a few swapped nouns
- prefer one sharp observation over three "good marketing" sentences
- keep the recipient at the center

For internal docs and strategy writing:
- remove fake authority language
- remove consultant-speak
- keep the point intact but say it like someone with judgment wrote it

## Hard rule

If the rewrite becomes cleaner but more lifeless, keep going. The goal is not "less AI-looking text." The goal is text that has an actual pulse.
