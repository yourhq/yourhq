HQ hosted gives you the full platform without managing any infrastructure. You sign up, we provision a workspace, and you're ready to create agents in under two minutes.

<Info>
  This guide is for the hosted version at [yourhq.ai](https://yourhq.ai). If you want to self-host HQ on your own machine, see the [self-host quickstart](/getting-started/quickstart) instead.
</Info>

## Step 1 — Sign up

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to yourhq.ai and click Get started">
    You'll see a short onboarding flow — three screens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your details">
    - **Your name** — how agents will address you
    - **Workspace name** — whatever you'd like to call it (e.g. your company or project name)
    - **Context** — pick the preset that best matches what you'll use HQ for. This seeds your pipeline stages, fields, and streams. You can change everything later.

    Presets: Growth, Sales, Recruiting, Clients, Personal, Other.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and pay">
    Review your workspace details and proceed to checkout. HQ is **$30/month per workspace**, billed through Stripe.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Step 2 — Watch your workspace come online

After payment, you'll see a live provisioning screen. Each step checks off as it completes:

1. **Creating database** — a dedicated Supabase project, just for you
2. **Applying schema** — tables, functions, and security policies
3. **Setting up your workspace** — your account, pipeline, and fields
4. **Starting agent runtime** — a sandboxed environment where your agents run
5. **Connecting gateway** — the runtime registers with your workspace

This usually takes about a minute. When it's done, you'll see **"Your workspace is ready"** and a button to sign in.

---

## Step 3 — Sign in

Check your email for a sign-in link. Click it — you'll land directly in your dashboard. No password needed; HQ uses magic links.

<Tip>
  The link expires after 1 hour. If it's gone, go to [yourhq.ai/login](https://yourhq.ai/login) and request a new one.
</Tip>

---

## Step 4 — Connect a model provider

Go to **Settings → Connections** and add a provider. Your agents need a model to think with.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="OpenAI (recommended for first try)">
    1. Go to [platform.openai.com/api-keys](https://platform.openai.com/api-keys)
    2. Create a new key
    3. Back in HQ: click **Add connection → OpenAI → API key**
    4. Paste your key and click **Connect**

    Cost: ~$0.01–0.10 per agent conversation depending on the model.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Anthropic">
    1. Go to [console.anthropic.com/settings/keys](https://console.anthropic.com/settings/keys)
    2. Create a new key
    3. Back in HQ: click **Add connection → Anthropic → API key**
    4. Paste your key and click **Connect**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Other providers">
    HQ supports 15+ providers: Gemini, DeepSeek, Mistral, Groq, OpenRouter, and more. Click **Add connection** to see the full list.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Info>
  HQ never touches your API keys for billing. You pay the model provider directly — HQ just routes calls through your key.
</Info>

## Step 5 — Create your first agent

Go to **Agents → New Agent**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a template">
    Choose a role. **Assistant** is a good general-purpose starting point. Each template comes with pre-loaded skills, memory structure, and a personality.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set identity">
    Give your agent a name. The slug is auto-generated. You can add an emoji and description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a channel">
    How you'll talk to your agent:
    - **Telegram** — easiest. Create a bot at [@BotFather](https://t.me/botfather) and paste the token.
    - **Discord** — create a bot in the [Discord Developer Portal](https://discord.com/developers/applications) and paste the token.
    - **None** — skip channels and interact through tasks, automations, and the remote desktop.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create">
    Click **Create Agent**. HQ provisions the agent on your runtime — this takes about 60 seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pair your channel (Telegram / Discord only)">
    Send a DM to your bot. It replies with a 6-digit pairing code. Paste that code in the agent's detail page → **Pairing Code** → click **Approve**. Your next message triggers the agent.
  </Step>
</Steps>
